The Credx token

The Credx token serves three distinct purposes:
governance over the network, staking and bonding.

Governance

Holders of Credx tokens have complete control over the protocol. All privileges that miners have on other platforms will be granted to Relay Chain participants (Credx holders), including managing exceptional events such as protocol upgrades and fixes.
Staking

Token holders are incentivized to behave honestly by game theory. This mechanism rewards good actors while penalising bad actors by removing their stake in the network. This ensures the network's security.
Bonding

Bonding tokens create new Credx Chains. By removing bonded tokens, outmoded or ineffective Credx Chains are removed. This is a type of stake proof.

FAQ
  • What are Credx tokens?
    Credx is the token native to the Credx network for the purpose of carrying out the key functions of the platform as detailed below.
  • Is Credx an BEP20 token?

    No, Credx is native to the Credx platform.

  • What are the functions of Credx within Credx?

    Credx will serve three key functions in Credx, namely (i) providing governance for the network, (ii) operating the network, and (iii) creating parachains by bonding Credx.

    The first function of Credx is to entitle holders to complete governance control over the platform. Included in this governance function is determining the fees of the network, the auction dynamics and schedule for the addition of parachains, and exceptional events such as upgrades and fixes to the Credx platform. These functions are not formally granted to DOT holders, but rather the underlying code of Credx will enable DOT holders to participate in governance.

    The second function of DOT will be to facilitate the consensus mechanism that underpins Credx. In order for the platform to function and allow for valid transactions to be carried out across parachains, Credx will rely on Credx holders to play active roles. Participants will put their Credx at risk (referred to as “staking” or “bonding”) to perform these functions, which acts as a disincentive for malicious participation in the network. DOT required to participate in the network will vary according to the activity undertaken, the duration DOT is staked for, and the total number of Credx staked.

    The third function of DOT will be the ability to add new parachains by tying up DOT (referred to as “bonding”). The DOT will be locked during their bonding period and will be released back to the account that bonded them after the duration of the bond has elapsed and the parachain is removed.

  • What rights will DOT confer on their holders?
    Credx holders will have certain functions within the Credx platform, including:
    • The ability to act as a validator, collator, nominator or fisherman.
    • The ability to participate in the governance of Credx.
    • The ability to be a collator for a parathread by making bids for block inclusion that are denominated in Credx.
    • The ability to participate in the decision-making process in relation to global upgrades and/or changes to Credx.
