Any type of data across any type of blockchain

Credx is a network protocol that allows arbitrary data to be transferred across blockchains rather than just tokens.

Credx is thus a true multi-chain application environment, allowing for cross-chain registries and cross-chain computation.

Credx can move data between public, open, permissionless blockchains and private, permissioned blockchains.

This enables the development of applications that retrieve permissioned data from a private blockchain and use it on a public blockchain. For example, a private, permissioned academic records chain at a school could send proof to a degree-verification smart contract on a public chain.

Network Glossary

Credx connects a network of disparate blockchains known as Credx Chains and Credx Threads. The Credx Relay Chain connects and secures these chains. They can also communicate with other networks via bridges.

Connecting the dots

Relay Chain

Credx's heart, in charge of the network's shared security, consensus, and cross-chain interoperability.

Credx Chain

Sovereign blockchains with their own tokens that can be optimised for specific use cases.

credx threads

Credx Chains-style, but with a pay-as-you-go model. More cost-effective for blockchains that do not require continuous network connectivity.

Bridges

Allow Credx Chains and Credx Threads to communicate with external networks such as Binance and Tron.

Consensus Roles

Nominators

Choose trustworthy validators and stake dots to secure the Relay Chain.

Validators

Stake CREDX to secure the relay chain, validate proofs from collators, and participate in consensus with other validators.

Collators

Maintain shards by collecting shard transactions from users and producing validator proofs.

Governance Roles

Council Members

Elected to represent passive stakeholders in two primary governance roles: proposing referendums and vetoing referendums that are dangerous or malicious.

Technical Committee

Credx is made up of teams that are actively working to build it. Can propose emergency referenda with the council for expedited voting and implementation.

Built with the best technology

Credx's relay chain is built with Substrate, a blockchain-building framework derived from Credx Technologies' experience building Binance, Tron, and enterprise blockchains.

Credx's state machine is written in WebAssembly (Wasm), a fast virtual environment. Wasm is being developed by major corporations such as Google, Apple, Microsoft, and Mozilla, which have created a large ecosystem of support for the standard.

Credx's networking makes use of libp2p, a cross-platform network framework designed for peer-to-peer applications. Libp2p, which is positioned to be the standard for future decentralised applications, manages peer discovery and communication in the Credx ecosystem.

Credx's runtime environment is written in Rust, C++, and Golang, making it available to a wide range of developers.

Processing transactions in parallel

Credx Chains are blockchains that connect to Credx. They will have characteristics tailored to their use cases as well as the ability to manage their own governance.

Credx Chain interactions are processed in parallel, allowing for highly scalable systems.

Transactions can be distributed across chains, allowing many more transactions to be processed in the same amount of time.

Groundbreaking Technology

Credx is addressing many of the issues that have stymied blockchain technology thus far — all in one place, without compromising.

A better security model

Blockchains compete for resources to secure their networks in both Proof-of-Work and Proof-of-Stake systems, and blockchains are easily attacked until they develop a significant community to support their network.

Credx takes a different approach, allowing blockchains to pool their security, which means that the security of the blockchains is aggregated and applied to all.

Blockchain developers can secure their blockchain from the start by connecting to Credx.

A green, energy-efficient protocol

When blockchain wastes so much energy, it's difficult to support it as the next technological revolution. Credx is intended to power the next big wave of web innovation while avoiding the high energy consumption of traditional proof-of-work blockchains.

Credx's Nominated Proof-of-Stake (NPoS) model consumes a fraction of the energy used by traditional blockchains and has the lowest carbon footprint of the proof-of-stake protocols studied in recent research.

Transparent on-chain governance

Protocol updates are made fork-free through transparent on-chain voting, ensuring that protocol development never stalls due to a lack of a clear process. The relay chain employs an advanced governance mechanism that is intended to create a transparent, accountable, and binding process for resolving disputes and upgrading the network.

CREDX tokens are used to participate in governance decisions such as proposal tabling, voting, and bonding.

Credx Chains are free to create their own governance mechanisms, allowing for maximum freedom while minimising impact on other chains.

GRANDPA: a trustworthy consensus algorithm

For a more secure and resilient network, Credx employs its original GRANDPA (GHOST-based Recursive Ancestor Deriving Prefix Agreement) consensus.

GRANDPA can almost instantly finalise blocks in good network conditions. GRANDPA can finalise large numbers of blocks (theoretically millions) at once when bad network conditions, such as a network partition, resolve.

Roadmap

Credx v1's launch began in October 2020 with the Credx Relay Chain genesis block and ended in December 2020 with the release of Credx Chains. Several post-launch developments and enhancements are planned for 2022 and beyond.

What are teams building on Credx? Just a few examples:

Smart contract chains with WebAssembly smart contracts (Astar, Moonbeam)

Data curation networks that connect all file storage chains into curated data sets

Oracle chains that make off-chain data available to all contracts on the Polkadot network (ChainLink)

Identity chains that link accounts to a persistent identity and enable access to other parachains through fewer accounts (Kilt)

Financial chains that allow you to hold all your assets in one portfolio, including via bridges to Bitcoin, Ethereum, Bitcoin Cash, Litecoin and ZCash (Acala, Parallel Finance)

Internet of Things chains that set IoT standards for machine-to-machine communication (Robonomics)

Zero Knowledge privacy chains, or bridges to existing ZK-snarks chains

File storage chains that incentivize storing data on-chain

Bridge to Ethereum, allowing Ethereum smart contracts to interact with the Polkadot network
